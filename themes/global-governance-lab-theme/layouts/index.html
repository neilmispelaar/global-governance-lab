Run by the Institute for the Study of International Development. This Lab is bridging the gap between academics, practitioners and policy makers in Canada and globally on issues related to global governance.

About the Lab

The Global Governance Lab is housed at McGill Universityâ€™s Institute for the Study of International Development (ISID). It is facilitated by Dr. Sonia Laszlo, Lab Program Lead and Director at ISID.

Purpose of the Lab

  • Advance knowledge

    Advance knowledge creation, dissemination and outreach on three themes: governance, natural resource conflict, and migration and refugees.

  • Strengthen the evidence-base

    Strengthen the evidence-base on what works, and does not work, to fill the contemporary global governance void.

  • Understand the role of policy

    Understand how social and economic policy can help improve the situation of individuals in fragile environments.

  • Enable experts

    Enable researchers, policy makers and practitioners to promote sustainable, inclusive development and democratic governance.
